# The Dialectic Skill: A Comprehensive Overview

This is Claude Code's structured methodology for using paired AI agents as an "Electric Monks" system—a framework where two committed advocates argue opposing positions while you operate from a belief-free orchestrator role to perform structural contradiction analysis.

## Core Purpose

The skill addresses a fundamental cognitive bottleneck: once you believe something, you cannot simultaneously hold its negation at full strength. The Electric Monks carry belief loads on your behalf, freeing you to analyze the *structure* of contradictions rather than being trapped inside either position.

## Key Framework: Three Theories

**Rao's Artificial Belief System**: This isn't AI thinking for you—it's AI *believing* for you. The monks eliminate context-switching costs by carrying 100% of belief weight, like hydraulic controls that free a pilot's attention for better strategic choices.

**Hegel's Determinate Negation**: Contradictions resolve not through compromise but through "Aufhebung"—simultaneously canceling, preserving, and elevating positions into something neither party could have conceived alone. The specific way each position fails points toward what's missing.

**Boyd's Destruction and Creation**: Genuine novelty requires shattering existing conceptual domains and reconnecting parts with material from outside domains—you cannot synthesize genuinely new understanding by recombining within the same frame.

## Seven-Phase Process

1. **Elenctic Interview & Research**: Surface hidden assumptions and ground the monks in evidence
2. **Generate Monk Prompts**: Two fully-committed belief positions, calibrated to your cognitive burden
3. **Spawn Electric Monks**: Separate agents argue from conviction—no hedging allowed
4. **Determinate Negation**: Structural analysis of where each position fails specifically
5. **Sublation**: Synthesis that transforms the question itself
6. **Validation & Auditing**: Monks evaluate elevation; hostile auditor finds flaws
7. **Recursion**: Each synthesis generates new contradictions—typically 2-3 more rounds for depth

## When to Use

- Stress-testing ideas against strongest counterarguments
- Resolving genuine tensions where tradeoffs are unclear
- Building deeper mental models, not just picking answers
- High-stakes decisions where understanding matters more than speed

## Critical Operating Principles

**Anti-hedging is functional requirement**: A monk that says "both sides have merit" has failed its job—you pick up the dropped belief weight and lose the orchestrator position.

**First round is calibration**: Real breakthroughs emerge in rounds 2-3 after the process digs past surface framings into deeper tensions.

**Synthesis must have emergent structure**: If every element traces back to one monk or the other, you've compromised rather than created.

**The queue is an orientation library**: Unresolved contradictions form a map of intellectual territory—pre-positioned reorientations you can snap into later.

## Practical Notes

- Requires strongest available model with extended thinking across all phases
- Heterogeneous model families (different AI providers) increase creative output through decorrelation
- External-research domains need ~150-250K tokens for initial research; personal domains trade this for deeper interviews
- Recursive rounds often need fresh research as each synthesis opens conceptual space previously invisible
- Total cost: ~300-400K tokens for one round plus recursion in research-heavy domains; 100-200K in personal domains

The skill treats genuine intellectual friction as an asset to be systematically analyzed rather than resolved through compromise or information consumption.
